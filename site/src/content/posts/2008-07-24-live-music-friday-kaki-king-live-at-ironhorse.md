Welcome back to another [Live Music Friday](/category/live-music-friday/) here at **Burgo’s Blog**. For today’s post – and largely due to the fact that I’ve just watched “[August Rush](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00133KFGW?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B00133KFGW)![](/e/ir)” again – I thought I would post something slightly different; **a 2004 show, where guitar virtuoso Kaki King performed, lived at the Ironhorse.**

![Live Music Friday - Kaki King, live at Ironhorse](/wp-content/uploads/03.jpg)

Now, I’m pretty sure this show isn’t going to appeal to everyone. It’s not always “easy listening” music. But, especially if you’re a guitarist, I implore you to listen to this show.

**[  
Kaki King](http://www.kakiking.com/)** is one of those rare musicians who comes along, **and you just know there’s something otherworldly there**. **There’s an emotion, and an intelligence behind her compositions that is few and far between in current music.** With a sound that involves fret-tapping, slap bass, percussive guitars and – lately – sound layering and looping, Kaki King is reminscent of Michael Hedges, Preston Reed and [Andy McKee](/2008/01/06/andy-mckee-is-a-better-guitarist-than-you/).  
**In short… that’s bloody heady company**.

**In the last 2 years, particularly, Kaki has been getting some major mainstream exposure**; not only is her music featured in the film “August Rush” – in fact, she was actually Freddy Highmore’s “action hands” – but she also contributed tunes to the, quite frankly, brilliant “[Into the Wild](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000ZN802W?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000ZN802W)![](/e/ir)” soundtrack. **Add into that mix some major support from the Foo Fighters, and in particular Dave Grohl, and you can see this girl’s ready to hit it large.**

  
While in the last few years, Kaki King has made an obvious effort to step back from the “solo instrumental artist” label that was beginning to attach itself to her – [take one listen to her new single, “**Pull Me Out Alive**“](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pVYp2sgA9M0), from her new album, “**Dreaming Of Revenge**“, and you’ll see what I mean –  **this show from 2004 was more in her original style. There’s minimal vocals, acoustic guitar… and it remains one of my favourites.**

  
If nothing else, make sure that you listen to the track, “Lies”**. It’s pure poetry.**

**  
Listen. Love. Support.**
