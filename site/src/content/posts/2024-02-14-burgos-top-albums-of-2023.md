Yes, I've been absent again. Two years since my last post. Sorry about that. I did actually put together a *"Burgo's Top Albums of 2022"* with all the intent of posting it here last year, but life got in the way. If you're interested in what made the cut, you can still find the playlist on Spotify — but for now, let's talk about what made me sit down and actually open the laptop in February of 2024.

2023 was, in a word, *generous*. Generous with second chances (hi, Killers; hi, Aimee Mann), generous with reinvention (Zach Bryan went from honky-tonk patron saint to stadium-fillers in twelve months flat), and generous with new voices that already sound like they've been writing for a decade.

> If 2022 was the year I forgot to listen, 2023 was the year music came back and shook me by the shoulders.

The list below is in no particular order, mostly because ranking the records that meant the most to you in a year feels like ranking your friends. I won't do it. But I will tell you why each one earned its spot.

## Jason Isbell & The 400 Unit — Weathervanes

He's done it again. Eleven songs that read like short stories, and a band that's never sounded tighter. *King Of Oklahoma* alone earned this its spot.

Then there's Zach Bryan. I'll be honest: I was a skeptic. The self-titled record turned me. Quiet songs, loud songs, three-minute songs that feel like they've been around forever. *I Remember Everything* with Kacey Musgraves was the duet of the year.

And then, somewhere between the Tommy Prine debut and the Lori McKenna record, I realised I'd been listening to a lot of country in 2023. That's fine. Sometimes the year picks the genre for you.

## The honourable mentions.

Records I almost included, in no order, and with apologies to anyone whose record I left out because I haven't heard it yet: Wilder Woods' *FEVER / SKY*, Darlingside's *Everything Is Alive*, Bill Orcutt's *Jump On It*, and Noname's *Sundial* — the last one mostly because I'm still figuring out what I think about it, which is usually a good sign.

That's it. That's the post. See you in a year (or three).

— Burgo
