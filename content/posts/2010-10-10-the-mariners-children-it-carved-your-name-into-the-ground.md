[**The Mariner’s Children**](http://www.myspace.com/marinerschildren) are one of those happy finds that you just manage to stumble across, you know? I was trying out the [Facebook page browse function](http://www.facebook.com/pages/browser.php) (warning: time waster!) and came across a page with an interesting name: [The Mariner’s Children](http://www.facebook.com/pages/The-Mariners-Children/200460084420?v=wall).

After clicking through, there was a video posted to the wall that absolutely blew me away. A live recording of “Golden Pine”, the string interplay and slow-burn of a start had me hooked right away.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/886j2gUzLo0" title="YouTube video" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe>

Looking into the band a bit more, it turns out they’re releasing a new EP called “New Moore Island” on 1 November, and giving away a track from the EP, called “It Carved Your Name Into The Ground”.

**And damned if it isn’t awesome.**

It’s got that same kind of foot-stamping rhythm that [Mumford and Sons](/tag/mumford-and-sons/) have made so popular, but there’s something more… I don’t know, freeform about it? It’s relentless, but at the same time, wild.

In other words, it’s exactly my kind of tune.  
**Listen. Love. Support.**

**Listen: [The Mariner’s Children – It Carved Your Name Into The Ground](http://melodywritings.com/burgotunes/TheMarinersChildren-ItCarvedYourNameintotheGround.mp3)**

“New Moore Island” is out November 1.
