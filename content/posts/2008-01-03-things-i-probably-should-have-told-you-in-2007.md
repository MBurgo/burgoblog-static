In the course of writing this blog, I come across a lot – and I mean, a **_lot_** – of music. Some of which I’ll blog about, some of which never seems to get its own post.

I was organising my files recently, and came across **plenty of songs that I probably should have told you all about in 2007**, but which – for whatever reason – never quite spurred me to write a full post. So, in clearing out these files into the “2007 Releases” folder, I thought I would do a bit of a roundup post, and combine them all here.

In other words, please note that this is **NOT** a “**best of 2007**” compilation; **it is simply a post of good songs from 2007, that you should hear.**

With that out of the way, let’s get onto the music!

(Once again… to all new visitors, simply click the “play” icon next to the song name to listen)

**All The Way Down by Glen Hansard**

![Glen Hansard - All The Way Down](/wp-content/glen-hansard2.jpg)

One of the most talked-about soundtracks of 2007 – and deservedly so – was the **[Once](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000PFU7OO?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000PFU7OO)![](/e/ir) OST**. Comprised of songs by the films two stars (**Glen Hansard** and **Marketa Irglova**), the soundtrack was hauntingly beautiful.

This song, “**All the way down**”, along with “**Leave**”, was perhaps my favourite of the album. The aching line “_You have broken me, all the way down_” easily wins my award for most breaking lyric of the year.

You can still listen to the entire soundtrack at the [Once movie site](http://www.foxsearchlight.com/once/).

I managed to catch Hansard with his band, The Frames, when they were opening for Bob Dylan on his recent tour out here. They were amazing, and confirmed to me that **the man is easily one of the most important voices in modern music**.

Listen: [Glen Hansard – All The Way Down](/wp-content/uploads/All_The_Way_Down.mp3)

**I Was A Daughter by Basia Bulat**

![Basia Bulat - I was a Daughter](/wp-content/basia-bulat2.jpg)

I’m still surprised that this didn’t make an entry. At 24, **Basia Bulat** is another artist that I should add to my “[Artists to watch for in 2008](/category/artists-to-watch-for-in-2008/)” category.

**Her youth belies an intensely creative soul**, and an artist who is totally unafraid. Obvious influences include Joni Mitchell (to whom she has been compared more than once), but Basia displays a voice and style that is all her own; sometimes fragile and whispy, and at other times strong, passionate and sultry, Bulat is the master of changing her vocal delivery to suit the content of the song.

This song, “**I was a Daughter**”, is taken from her “**[Oh My Darling](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000N6TZKW?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000N6TZKW)**![](/e/ir)” debut album, and was **easily my debut album of the year.**

Oh. And did I mention she’s my musical crush for 2007?  
She is.

Listen: [Basia Bulat – I Was A Daughter](/wp-content/uploads/I_Was_A_Daughter.mp3)

**No One’s Gonna Love You by Band of Horses**

![Band of Horses - No one’s Gonna Love You](/wp-content/band-of-horses.jpg)

A little Morning Jacket, some Shins, and a hint of Built to Spill, and you’ll end up with something like **Band of Horses**. This song, taken from their “**[Cease to Begin](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000UVPKEU?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000UVPKEU)**![](/e/ir)” album, is more emotionally direct than anything from their 2006 debut, “**[Everything All the Time](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000E6GBV2?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000E6GBV2)**![](/e/ir)”, but despite the desperately cheese-laden title line, **the song is a gorgeous slow-jam**, with the cracking “_If things start splitting at the seams and now / It’s tumbling down hard_” showcasing Bridwell’s expressive voice.

Listen: [Band of Horses – No One’s Gonna Love You](/wp-content/uploads/No_Ones_Gonna_Love_You.mp3)

**Heretics by Andrew Bird**

![Andrew Bird - Armchair Apocrypha](/wp-content/andrew-bird-armchair.jpg)

Well! Didn’t **Andrew Bird** grow up in 2007? The rueful reflection of Bird’s 2007 release, “**[Armchair Apocrypha](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000MV9A1C?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000MV9A1C)**![](/e/ir)”, and in particular, this song, “**Heretics**”, had me more excited about Bird than his past releases.

The off-kilter pop songs are still there, but **there’s something _more_ about his reflection of the world as something beautiful, yet unsettling, in this album than his previous.**  
Here’s to more of this.

Listen: [Andrew Bird – Heretics](/wp-content/uploads/Heretics.mp3)

**Listened On by Lightning Dust**

![Lightning Dust - Listened On](/wp-content/lightning-dust2.jpg)

Black Mountaineers Amber Webber and Joshua Wells side project, **Lightning Dust**, came out of nowhere and knocked me on my ass. While Black Mountain displays it’s 60s psychedelic love, Lightning Dust is far more sparse, and far more reflective.

This track, “**Listened On**”, from their debut album (fittingly titled “**[Lightning Dust](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000PA9PUQ?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000PA9PUQ)**![](/e/ir)“), **is ghostly, quivering, and beautiful.**

Listen: [Lightning Dust – Listened On](/wp-content/uploads/Lightning_Dust.mp3)

**Nothing’s Lost by Patrick Park**

![Patrick Park - Nothing’s Lost](/wp-content/patrick-park.jpg)

I came across **Patrick Park** relatively late, with his second album, “**[Loneliness Knows My Name](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0000A0DVY?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B0000A0DVY)**![](/e/ir)”, but at that time was undecided as to whether he lived up to the hype or not.  
With his 2007 release, “**[Everyone’s in Everyone](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000SO7IVW?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000SO7IVW)![](/e/ir)**”, however, this doubt is solidly put to rest. Patrick Park is one of those artists who can reflect your emotions is a single line.

This track, “**Nothing’s Lost**”, takes your head against it’s shoulder and **consoles you with it’s lulling rhythm**.

Listen: [Patrick Park – Nothing’s Lost](/wp-content/uploads/Nothings_Lost.mp3)

**Your Rocky Spine by Great Lake Swimmers**

![Great Lake Swimmers - Your Rocky Spine](/wp-content/great-lake-swimmers-2.jpg)

With it’s Nickle Creek-like tones, **this was one of the singles of the year for me**. A mixture of indie-folk, roots melodies and alt-country, the music of **Great Lake Swimmers** is at once graceful, comforting, full of loss and isolation, and ridiculously beautiful.

**An ethereal whisper of an album that is hauntingly beautiful**, “**[Ongiara](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000OHZKB8?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000OHZKB8)**![](/e/ir)” (from which this song, “**Your Rocky Spine**” is taken) is a must-listen from 2007.

Listen: [Great Lake Swimmers – Your Rocky Spine](/wp-content/uploads/Your_Rocky_Spine.mp3)

**Skinny Love by Bon Iver**

![Bon Iver - Skinny Love](/wp-content/bon-iver3.jpg)

**Skinny Love** by **Bon Iver** is a song that grows in power the more you listen to it; as if, with each individual listen, it breaks a little more of you off. I’ve heard it described as a “_quiet, gentle punch to the heart_”, and I couldn’t put it better myself.

**A song of desperation, loneliness, and loss such as this one resonates deep in your heart**; listen to his plaintive “I told you” lines, and you’ll know what I mean.

The album from which this is taken, “**[For Emma, Forever Ago](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0011HF6GE?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B0011HF6GE)![](/e/ir)**” is one for the weepy folk-lover in all of us.

Listen: [Bon Iver – Skinny Love](/wp-content/uploads/Skinny_Love.mp3)

**Lake Michigan by Rogue Wave**

![Rogue Wave - Lake Michigan](/wp-content/roguewave2.jpg)

2007 saw **Rogue Wave** release their third-studio album, and the first for Jack Johnson’s Brushfire Fairytales label, “**[Asleep at Heaven’s Gate](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000UZ4G6Y?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000UZ4G6Y)**![](/e/ir)”; hopefully, this will be the one that catapaults them to the masses.

This album has a shimmer and jangle that wasn’t as prevalent in their previous releases; and **there is something ridiculously catchy about this handclapping single, Lake Michigan.**

Listen: [Rogue Wave – Lake Michigan](/wp-content/uploads/Lake_Michigan.mp3)

**No Cars Go by Arcade Fire**

![Arcade Fire - No Cars Go](/wp-content/arcadefire_hi.jpg)

I know, I know; **Arcade Fire**? How much more mainstream can this blog get?

Seriously though, Arcade Fire’s 2007 release, “**[Neon Bible](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000MGUZM0?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000MGUZM0)![](/e/ir)**”, _was_ a ridiculously good album. Coming off their _scorcher_ debut, “**[Funeral](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0002IVN9W?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B0002IVN9W)**![](/e/ir)”, most critics weren’t expecting them to follow up with something as worthy as this.

While Neon Bible certainly sees the band taking on some big issues (particularly the religion questioning “Antichrist Television Blues”), it never lets you forget that, **ultimately, Arcade Fire are about celebration**.

Never more evident is this than on the single, “**No Cars Go**”. Spine-tingling, wailing vocals, combined with drumming that punches the song along, and instrumentals that build into an almost film-worthy epic ending show that Arcade Fire are here to stay.

Listen: [Arcade Fire – No Cars Go](/wp-content/uploads/No_Cars_Go.mp3)

**Sun In An Empty Room by The Weakerthans**

![The Weakerthans - Sun in an Empty Room](/wp-content/weakerthans.jpg)

Well, honestly… when you get down to it, when have **The Weakerthans** _ever_ disappointed us? 2007 saw them release the excellent “**[Reunion Tour](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000UGG3M8?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000UGG3M8)![](/e/ir)**” album, which **I cannot recommend more.** In fact, I’ll rather let Paste Magazine’s review speak for me:

_“These are songs of brutal beauty, little rock n roll vignettes that perfectly capture the malaise of the peculiar, disorienting times in which we live.”_

This track, “**Sun in an empty room**”, highlights John K Samson’s incredible command of narrative lyrics; indeed, as much as he protests against the label, Samson really is an indie poet laureate. **His weary, wry observations possess an underdog nobility that you’d be hard pressed to find elsewhere.**

Listen: [The Weakerthans – Sun In An Empty Room](/wp-content/uploads/Sun_In_An_Empty_Room.mp3)

**Tonight by Hard-Fi**

![Hard Fi - Tonight](/wp-content/hard-fi2.jpg)

I’ll be honest: **Hard-Fi**’s 2007 release, “**[Once Upon a Time In the West](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000UYX594?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000UYX594)![](/e/ir)**” didn’t quite live up to their debut album “**[Stars of CCTV](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0009OU2M8?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B0009OU2M8)![](/e/ir)**” for me. There’s something about the anger in their songs that seems to have lost its authenticity for me.

However, that’s not to say that the album was totally without merit; this single, “**Tonight**”, was one of the better products from the album, and with **it’s ghostly strings and chant-along chorus hints that there may be life in the boys yet**.

Listen: [Hard-Fi – Tonight](/wp-content/uploads/Tonight.mp3)

**Impossible Germany by Wilco**

![Wilco - Impossible Germany](/wp-content/wilco.jpg)

While **Wilco**’s 2007 release, “**[Sky Blue Sky](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000NVIGC0?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000NVIGC0)**![](/e/ir)” was widely criticised by fans as being a back-slide by Jeff Tweedy into the classic-rock gospel, it produced perhaps one of my favourite Wilco tracks of all-time, “**Impossible Germany**”.

**It’s a warm and inviting listen, one with jazzy chords filtered through the ever-present alt.country guitar licks**. There’s no noise here, no strange distortions that have peppered Wilco’s latest releases… and I for one think the song is perhaps one of the most powerful Wilco songs for it.

Peaceful on the surface, demented under the water; this is Wilco at it’s best.

Listen: [Wilco – Impossible Germany](/wp-content/uploads/Impossible_Germany.mp3)

**Murderer by Low**

![Low - Murderer](/wp-content/low2.jpg)

**Low** is not, and never _has been_, for everyone. Pretty much defining the genre of “slowcore” with their appearance in 1993, they have, however, certainly displayed their longevity.

In 2005, Low attempted to break away from their slowcore label with the harder, bigger sound of their “**[The Great Destroyer](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000777J9G?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000777J9G)![](/e/ir)**” album. Considering the mixed reception to _that_ album, most thought the band would return to their signature sound. **Most thought wrong**.  
However, just as the album is not a return to their signature sound, neither is it a continuation of _The Great Destroyer_. Rather, **this album strips the songs to the bones and sinews; it’s something else entirely**. Violent, scary and beautiful, with this album, “**[Drums and Guns](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000MV8CSO?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000MV8CSO)![](/e/ir)**”, Low show that they’re still kicking.

Listen: [Low – Murderer](/wp-content/uploads/Murderer.mp3)

**Boy With A Coin by Iron & Wine**

![Iron and Wine - Boy With The Coin](/wp-content/iron-and-wine2.jpg)

Undoubtedly **one of the albums of the year for me**, **Iron and Wine**’s  
“**[The Shepherd’s Dog](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000TQZ7O4?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000TQZ7O4)**![](/e/ir)” displayed Sam Beam’s steady evolution from lo-fi folkster to refined popsmith.

This stonking single, “**Boy With A Coin**” showcases a far more lush and full sound that is employed on this album (surely a product of Sam’s collaboration with Calexico in 2005), and is **easily one of my favourite Iron and Wine songs**.

Listen: [Iron & Wine – Boy With A Coin](/wp-content/uploads/Boy_With_A_Coin.mp3)

**Thrash Unreal by Against Me!**

![Against Me! - Thrash Unreal](/wp-content/againstme.jpg)

Included more in this list due to the fact that **Thrash Unreal** could prove to be one of the most important songs of **Against Me!**’s career.

2007 saw Against Me! release their first major label debut, “**[New Wave](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000QFCD0Y?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000QFCD0Y)**![](/e/ir)”, which is in stark contrast to their previously strong DIY ethos.

**Lead single “Thrash Unreal” unveils a new penchant for pop sensibility, and the song shows the band’s strength for anthemic, infectious choruses.** And – thankfully – Tom Gabel’s harsh growl of a voice remains intact here, and there still seems enough of the Against Me! punk left in the guys.  
That said, have Against Me! sold out by their move away from Fat Wreck Chords, and recording with this far, _FAR_ more polished sound?

Time will tell (I’m hoping not), but there’s no denial that the 2007 release, “New Wave”, and it’s lead single Thrash Unreal **could make or break the band amongst it’s fans.**

Let’s hope there’s still a “**I Still Love You Julie**” song left in the lads.

Listen: [Against Me! – Thrash Unreal](/wp-content/uploads/Thrash_Unreal.mp3)

**My Mind by Portugal. The Man**

![Portugal. The Man - My Mind](/wp-content/portugal-the-man.jpg)

I’ve heard many people describe “**Portugal. The Man**” as a more accessible Mars Volta, and I think it’s a fair description; elements of The Mars Volta, and even The White Stripes pop up in their 2007 release, “**[Church Mouth](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000RGSOMI?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000RGSOMI)![](/e/ir)**”

**With its psychedelic, bluesy prog rock tones, the album is one that you’ll either love or hate**; but like the below single, “**My Mind**”, I encourage you to give it a few spins before making up your mind.

Listen: [Portugal. The Man – My Mind](/wp-content/uploads/My_Mind.mp3)

**Baltimore by Stephen Malkmus and the Jicks**

![Stephen Malkmus and The Jicks - Baltimore](/wp-content/stephenmalkmusandthejicks2.jpg)

Yes, you read that right. **Stephen Malkmus**, former frontman for all-time greats “The Pavements” was back in 2007, and making delicious noise with his new band, **The Jicks**.

Still all crunch and fuzz – but with elegant melodies and flourishes throughout – and laced with Malkmus’ literate lyrics and tender, crackling tone, the song “**Baltimore**” is a godsend to Malkmus fans.

Listen: [Stephen Malkmus and The Jicks – Baltimore](/wp-content/uploads/Baltimore.mp3)

**Nikole by George Stanford**

![George Stanford - Nikole](/wp-content/georgestanford2.jpg)

Another artist who will undoubtedly become huge in 2008, 2007 saw the debut of **George Stanford**. Endearingly simply titled “**The EP**” George Stanford’s 5-song 2007 release must simply be a taster for a 2008 full album release; and what a tasty taster it is.

This single, “**Nikole**”, showcases a piano balladry that fans of Ben Folds are sure to fall in love with.

**If there’s one artist who debuted in 2007 who I would place a wager on becoming a household name in 2008, I would wager on it being George Stanford**. If it’s not, it would simply be a crime.

Listen: [George Stanford – Nikole](/wp-content/uploads/Nikole.mp3)

**Ordinary by The Alternate Routes**

![The Alternate Routes - Ordinary](/wp-content/alternateroutes.jpg)

From the opening lines of “_I’ve been wasting my days good and reckless and true, I have danced in the dark at the edge of the water, swingin my hips at the black and the blue…_” you know that **The Alternate Routes** are something special.

Sounding alternately like Ryan Adams during the verses (and, interestingly, current Cardinals drummer Brad Pemberton features on the skins here), and then David Gray in the chorus, **Tim Warren is one of the more exciting voices I heard this year.**

  
While this single, **Ordinary**, may give you a mainstream image of the band, I encourage you to listen to the entire album, “**[Good and Reckless and True](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000MG2LS6?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000MG2LS6)**![](/e/ir)”; **it is a grand, rootsy, absolutely golden album that warrants your attention**.

Listen: [The Alternate Routes – Ordinary](/wp-content/uploads/Ordinary.mp3)

**  
Fake Empire by The National**

![The National - Fake Empire](/wp-content/national_boxer.jpg)

**The National** came in in 2007 and absolutely blew us all away. Far more understated than their 2005 album, “**[Alligator](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0007LCNKM?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B0007LCNKM)**”, their 2007 release, “**[Boxer](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000O5AYCA?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000O5AYCA)**![](/e/ir)”, is perhaps the most fully realised release of the year by any artist.

A slow burn of an album, **Fake Empire** was the single from Boxer that grabbed my attention and held it; **an absolutely crushing single, if you didn’t hear it in 2007, grab this now.**

Listen: [The National – Fake Empire](/wp-content/uploads/Fake_Empire.mp3)

**200 More Miles by the Cowboy Junkies (featuring Ryan Adams)**

![Cowboy Junkies feat Ryan Adams - 200 More Miles](/wp-content/cowboy.jpg)

2007 saw the 20th anniversary of the massively important _Trinity Session_ album from the **Cowboy Junkies**, and to commemorate the occasion, the Junkies released a CD/DVD combo of performances in the same Toronto church where the original album was recorded.

As if _Golden Tiger_ wasn’t enough for us (but then again, when it comes to Ryan Adams, can we _EVER_ get enough?), the “**[Trinity Revisited](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0011XFOGU?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B0011XFOGU)![](/e/ir)**” recordings saw Ryan partner up with Cowboy Junkies to release this beautiful rendition of **200 More Miles.**

**Ryan was born to perform with the Cowboy Junkies**, and I couldn’t haven’t been more happy.

Listen: [Cowboy Junkies – 200 More Miles (feat Ryan Adams)](/wp-content/uploads/200_More_Miles.mp3)

**To The Dogs Or Whoever by Josh Ritter**

![Josh Ritter - To the Dogs or Whoever](/wp-content/joshritter.jpg)

**Josh Ritter**’s 2007 release, “**[The Historical Conquests of Josh Ritter (with Bonus EP)](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000TD9LE4?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000TD9LE4)![](/e/ir)**” was leagues from his 2006 album, “**[Animal Years](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000EOTV7U?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000EOTV7U)![](/e/ir)**”, and that’s saying something as I was a big fan of _that_ album.

**To me, Josh Ritter is one of the most underappreciated writers in the current rock/folk scene**; witty, wry, rueful and intelligent, it’s time that Josh Ritter got his dues from the general public and not just music blog writers.

That said, I’m not sure that this “**To the Dogs Or Whoever**” will be the single, to do it. **But damn it’s fun**.

Listen: [Josh Ritter – To The Dogs Or Whoever](/wp-content/uploads/To_The_Dogs.mp3)

**Four Winds by Bright Eyes**

![Bright Eyes - Four Winds](/wp-content/bright-eyes.jpg)

Who would have thought it? Despite the fairly lacklustre album released by **Bright Eyes** in 2007, “**[Cassadaga](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000N60HCW?ie=UTF8&tag=busblmuteraba-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B000N60HCW)**![](/e/ir)”, Conor Oberst somehow managed to get me interested in his material once again, thanks to the one redeeming single from that album, “**Four Winds**”.

You’ve brought me back from the brink of abandoning you Conor; and I hope that I stick around for a while.

Listen: [Bright Eyes – Four Winds](/wp-content/uploads/Four_Winds.mp3)
